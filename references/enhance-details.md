# Enhance Details, Emotion, and Texture

Use this module when the user asks to make a video more vivid, believable, emotional, cinematic, warm, healing, premium, grounded, or less empty.

## Concrete Detail Principle

Replace abstract claims with perceivable evidence. Prefer details the viewer can see, hear, feel, or imagine.

Useful detail types:

- 动作: opening a drawer, wiping a table, tying shoelaces, turning off a lamp.
- 物件: a coffee cup, wrinkled shirt, Lego dust, notebook corner, grocery receipt.
- 声音: water boiling, keyboard tapping, street rain, pan sizzling, elevator ding.
- 画面: morning light on the wall, an organized room corner, steam on a cup.
- 表情: a pause, a small smile, a tired blink, a relaxed exhale.
- 场景: commute under streetlights, kitchen at night, desk before work.
- 变化: messy to ordered, rushed to calm, stuck to moving, noisy to quiet.

## Detail Placement

- Put one strong concrete detail in the opening if the video needs a visual stop.
- Add one detail per chapter to support the chapter's task.
- Use details at emotional turns so the feeling is shown rather than explained.
- Avoid piling up beautiful shots that do not advance the theme.

## Emotional Line

Design emotion as a natural flow:

- 有点乱 -> 开始整理 -> 慢慢平静 -> 获得安定感
- 疲惫 -> 停下来观察 -> 找到一点秩序 -> 轻一点
- 好奇 -> 尝试 -> 发现问题 -> 得到方法
- 不确定 -> 行动 -> 看见变化 -> 形成信心

If emotion jumps, add a bridging action, sensory detail, or sentence that lets the viewer feel the transition.

## Pacing

Use a clear rhythm:

- Opening: faster, more direct, give a reason to stay.
- Middle: steadier, unfold process and details.
- Ending: slow down slightly, return to the theme and let the feeling land.

Clear pacing does not mean fast editing. It means every segment visibly moves the content forward.

## Audiovisual Consistency

Keep images, music, subtitles, and voiceover serving one atmosphere. Choose one main tone such as 松弛, 治愈, 复古, 清冷, 温暖, 生活感, 高级感, or 烟火气. If different elements point to different tones, recommend aligning the wording, music, and visuals.
