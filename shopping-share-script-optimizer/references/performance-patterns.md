# Performance Patterns

Use these patterns when comparing or improving the user's shopping-share scripts. They are distilled from the current folder tiers:

- `赞藏1000+`: strong samples.
- `赞藏500-1000`: mid-high samples.
- `<500`: comparison samples outside the tier folders.

## Strong Sample Signals (`赞藏1000+`)

Strong scripts tend to:

1. Move quickly from item to life scene.
   - Example pattern: `容量大 -> 相机、防晒、薄外套都能放 -> 周末/旅行会拿它`.
2. Describe perceptible results.
   - `第二天没洗头也有烫过的效果`.
   - `日常暴走也不会有问题`.
   - `喷在衣服上一两天还有味道`.
3. Include a clear reason for purchase.
   - `马上入秋了`.
   - `这个月底要去夏威夷`.
   - `大部分衣服都是黑白灰，所以想买配饰`.
4. Use concrete product functions.
   - side zipper, cup holder, zip pockets, arch support, water resistance, anti-wrinkle, storage capacity.
5. Add light life texture.
   - small jokes, small worries, already-used feedback, friend/family/gift context.

## Mid-High Sample Signals (`赞藏500-1000`)

Mid-high scripts often have good structure or useful information, but may be less sticky.

Common strengths:

- Clear grouping such as outerwear, tops, base layers, pants, shoes.
- Useful category knowledge, especially for creator gear and travel packing.
- More complete outfit descriptions and material details.

Common limits:

- Some sections sound like a neat product list.
- Personal scene density is lower than strong samples.
- Good words repeat without enough real outcome.
- Some drafts feel like material notes rather than finished spoken scripts.

## Lower Sample Signals (`<500`)

Lower-performing comparison drafts tend to:

- Include many items but fewer memory points.
- Stay at `版型好 / 颜色好看 / 很有质感 / 上身清爽`.
- Mention interesting items without explaining why the viewer should care.
- End without ranking, takeaway, or strongest recommendation.
- Change categories without a small transition or viewer promise.

## What Usually Lifts A Script

Prioritize these edits:

1. Add one usage scene to each important item.
2. Convert generic praise into a visible or tactile result.
3. Add one personal reason for buying or keeping the item.
4. Add one small honest note: defect, hesitation, comparison, or usage condition.
5. End with a short ranking or selection logic.

## Useful Comparison Lens

When reviewing a script, ask:

- Is this just a product description, or can the viewer picture it in the user's life?
- Does each item give the viewer a reason to save, ask, buy, compare, or remember?
- Which phrase would still make sense if said about any other item? Rewrite those first.
- Which item has the strongest scene or sensory result? Move it earlier or use it as the hook.
